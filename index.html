Welcome to the SoftMatter & Cavitation Lab

We are an experimental group at the Otto-von-Guericke-University. Our aim is to explore not so simple flows on small scales.

Previous Next

We work on problems such as

  • photo acoustics, time reversal acoustics, shockwaves in medicine

  • gas bubble dynamics, cavitation, nanobubbles, jets, fragmentation

  • elastic waves in tissue, and

  • heat transfer in boiling.

We are located at the Otto-von-Guericke-University, named after the founder of vacuum technolgy Otto von Guericke who built a first empty cavity.

We are looking for passionate new Master and Bachelor students, PhD and Postdocs students to join the team (more info) !

News

15. January 2018

Understanding droplet fragmentation is the key to smaller ICs. A collaboration with Slava Medvedev's group at Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology lead to better understanding how to shape droplets used in EUV light sources in Scientific Reports.

November 2017

We tested a novel boiling regime for applicability in space. The experiment was conducted with ESA through parabolic flights and in collaboration with Farhat's group at EPFL. Full success - it works!
